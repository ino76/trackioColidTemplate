TRAFFIC MODULE

Do you know how many people visited your store last month? Or how many people just passed by? No? We do! You always know how many potential customers you were able to attract to visit your store thanks to Trackio. It can also tell you if your new display window increased the store visits, how many customers are loyal to your brand or the amount of time people spent in your shop on average.

#1

Visits

A person who enters the store and spends there a certain amount of time is evaluated as a visitor and recorded in the Visits chart.

#2

People Passing by

people who passed by the store within predefined distance, up to 100 meters The People Passing by chart contains the visitors as well (Visits).

Conversion to Visitors

It presents how many percent of passers-by (People Passing by) became visitors (Visits), for example if it is every tenth visitor then the conversion is 10%.

People Passing by + Conversion to Visitors

A comparison of the People Passing by and the Conversion charts. The number of passers-by is illustrated on the left side while the percentage representing how many of them visited the store is shown on the right. For example, from the People Passing by + Conversion to Visitors chart, it is possible to see that more people visited the store on Friday than on Thursday. Although, the number of the passers-by was similar during both days.

#3

Returning Visitors

The number of visitors (Visits) who have returned is illustrated in the Returning Visitors chart.

Returning Visitors Percentage

The Returning Visitors Percentage chart shows how many percent of visitors (Visits) are returning (Returning Visitors).

Returning Visitors + Returning Visitors Percentage

The Returning Visitors + Returning Visitors Percentage chart compares the number of visitors who have visited the store (Returning Visitors) and the percentage of total visits of the store per given day.

#4

Average Visit Duration

The Average Visit Duration chart presents how many minutes on average the visitor spent in the store on a particular day. If the number is too low, it indicates a small necessity to stay in the store. Based on the goods offered, it can be a good or a bad sign (if it is a supply point, it will be a good sign as it means that the clearance is fast; on the other hand, if it happens in a store with a goods display, it will show incapability to attract visitors).

#5

Footfall

The Footfall chart allows to easily compare the visit rate at specific dates, hours and to identify repeating patterns. Thanks to this function, we can allocate more employees during the rush hours and on the contrary to save costs per employees when the extent of utilization is lower.

#6

Dwell Time Distribution

The Dwell Time Distribution chart shows the amount of time the customers spend at the store. If the number is too low, it indicates the small necessity to stay in the store. Based on the goods offered, it can be a good or a bad sign (if it is a supply point, it will be a good sign as it means that the clearance is fast; on the other hand, if it happens in a store with the goods display, it will show incapability to attract visitors).

#7

Average Footfall by Days of Week

The Average Footfall by Days of Week chart informs about the average number of visitors at selected period, days and hours. From the data, it can be deduced whether it will be profitable to extend the opening hours of the store.

#8

Average Number of Overlapping Visits

The Average Number of Overlapping Visits chart presents the number of the visitors which were at the store at the same time, i.e. how many of their visits overlapped. Based on this information, we can estimate how many employees we need to have at the store at specific time so that none of our customers would remain unnoticed. We cannot obtain this information from a mere number of visits, in an extreme case, for example, it can happen that among 2pm and 3pm, sixty visitors would come to the store, however everyone in a different minute therefore one employee would be enough to cover all the visitors. On contrary, if the visitors come in a groups of five, one employee will not be sufficient enough to take care of them.
