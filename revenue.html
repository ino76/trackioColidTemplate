#1: REVENUE MODUL

Number of Transactions

The Number of Transactions chart shows the number of transaction per particular day.

Visitor to Transaction Conversion

The Visitor to Transaction Conversion chart shows how many percent of visitors (Visit) bought something (made a transaction).

Number of Transactions + Visitor To Transaction Conversion

A comparison of the Number of Transactions and the Visitor to Transaction Conversion. The number of transactions is illustrated on the right, the information about the percentage of the visitors who made a transaction is shown on the left. The chart shows that on Friday June 9th it was possible to sell something to almost every other customer. In order to evaluate the data (ex. how many percent of the customers have bought something), we need to integrate records about the transactions from the cash register in a concrete store to our system. The data output can be used for the comparison of a performance of individual stores, e.g. if we have two stores with a comparable number of visitors however the sales are 20% higher in one of them, we can assume that lower sale could be caused by insufficient customer service or inappropriate location; subsequently the manager of the store can relocate employees to confirm the hypothesis and to optimize the profits at both stores.

People Passing by

People who passed by the store within 40 meters of any sensor. The People Passing chart contains the visitors as well (Visits).

Conversion to Visitors

It presents how many percent of passers-by (People Passing by) became visitors (Visits), for example if it is every tenth visitor then the conversion is 10%.

People Passing by + Conversion to Visitors

A comparison of the People Passing by and the Conversion charts. The number of passers-by is illustrated on the left side while the percentage representing how many of them visited the store is shown on the right. For example, from the People Passing by + Conversion to Visitors chart, it is possible to see that more people visited the store on Friday than on Thursday. Although, the number of the passers-by was similar during both days.

Revenue

The Revenue chart informs about revenue of a particular store per specific day.

Revenue per Visitor

The Revenue per Visitor chart illustrates the amount of money one customer (Visits) spent on average on a specific day. The data output can be used for the comparison of a performance of individual stores, e.g. if we have two stores with a comparable number of visitors however the average customer spends more money in one store than the other, it is possible that the lower sale could be caused by insufficient customer service or inappropriate location; subsequently the manager of the store can relocate employees to confirm the hypothesis and to optimize the profits at both stores.

Revenue + Revenue per Visitor

The Revenue + Revenue per Visitor chart compares the total revenue of the store (Revenue) with the average revenue of one customer (Revenue per Visitor). One-third more people came to store on Monday than on Sunday however the income was almost the same; it means that the employees persuaded more customers to buy a product during the shift on Sunday and therefore they were more effective (we could not find it out by simply comparing the turnovers of specific days). In comparison to the Average Revenue per Transaction chart, which distributes the revenue only to visitors who bought something, the Revenue + Revenue per Visitor chart assigns the revenue to all visitors regardless of whether they have made any transaction in the store.

Average Revenue per Transaction

The Average Revenue per Transaction chart shows how much the customer spent on average out of those who bought something. It calculates the revenue only among customers who bought something, in comparison to the Revenue per Visitor which distributes the revenue among all visitors.

Average Number of Transactions + Average Revenue per Transaction

The Average Number of Transactions + Average Revenue per Transaction chart compares the average number of transactions (Average Number of Transactions) with the average revenue per transaction (Average Revenue per Transaction), at specific hours across the selected time period e.g. one week.

Retention

The Retention table illustrates the amount of the returning visitors. Thanks to this information, we can track the loyalty (respectively the strength of the brand) and the effectivity of the marketing campaigns in selected month. We can see from the illustration table that the customers we gained in February are returning more often than those we gained in January, that means that in February, when the marketing campaign took place, a larger percentage of visitors returned and made bigger Revenue, although the total number of visits was lower than in January.
